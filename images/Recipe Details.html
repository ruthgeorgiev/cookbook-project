Vegan Chocolate Ganache Tart

Vegan Chocolate Ganache Tart, made with healthier gluten-free and vegan ingredients, and tastes ultra-decadent and delicious.he vegan chocolate ganache crust has no gluten and there is no egg or dairy in the chocolate filling. Finish with a crown of pistachios and fresh fig for an elegant vegan dessert. Pistachios give the crust a nice subtle flavor and look beautiful. Don‚Äôt have pistachios? You could use another nut if you like ‚Äì pecans and hazelnuts work really well. And if you have never made pastry, this vegan tart is one of the best ways to start your pie adventures. There‚Äôs no rolling required for the crust, just press, and bake. A tart pan with a removable bottom is really key for tarts made with crumb or shortbread style crusts. Being able to remove the sides makes it so much easier to slice the tart, and then remove each piece from the base.

Servings: 6-8
Total Mins: 45 min
Prep: 15 min
Cook: 30 min
Contains
Free From
Ingredient Amount
All purpose gluten-free flour290g
Almond meal160g
Coconut sugar3tbs
Salt1/2tsp
Vanilla bean1/4tsp
Vegan butter (cold)200g
Full fat coconut milk3 1/2 cups
Dark chocolate chips4 1/2 cups
Salt1/4tsp
Vanilla bean1/4tsp

Directions

  1. Add 290 grams all-purpose gluten-free flour into a bowl, 160 grams of almond meal, 3 tbsp of coconut sugar, 1/2 tsp of salt, and 1/4 tsp of vanilla then whisk to combine.
  2. Add cold pieces of vegan butter, using 200 grams of butter. Knead with your hands until the mixture sticks together. Spray the tart pan with olive oil or line with parchment paper to prevent sticking.
  3. Spread the crust mixture into a tart pan (30 cm round pan), press the mixture to the tart pan using your fingers, ensuring it's even everywhere.
  4. Place a piece of parchment paper on top of the prepared crust and fill with pie weights, rice, or chickpeas to prevent slumping or puffing. Bake for 12-15 minutes at 180 degrees Celsius.
  5. For the chocolate filling, heat 3.5 cups of full-fat coconut milk until simmering.
  6. Pour into a bowl with 4.5 cups of dark chocolate chips, wait 30 seconds then stir slowly until completely melted and smooth.
  7. Add 1/4 tsp of salt and vanilla, stirring until well combined.
  8. Remove the pie weights and bake the crust for another 5-10 minutes or until golden brown, dry, and firm. Then, allow to cool for about 10 minutes.
  9. Pour the chocolate filling into the crust, smoothing the top with a spoon or spatula.
  10. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to set completely. Then, remove from the tart pan and garnish with figs, berries, or pistachios.
